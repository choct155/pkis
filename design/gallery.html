Typical Sets — where the probability lives

Why, in high dimensions, almost all the mass sits in a thin shell away from the mode.

In one dimension the mode is where you'd bet. Add dimensions and the volume of that neighborhood collapses faster than its density grows — so the typical set, not the peak, is where samples actually fall.

mass in shell: 0.91
p(x) · Vol(shell)  peaks away from  argmax p(x)
The mode is a measure-zero trap: high density, vanishing volume. Sampling targets the product, not the peak.
