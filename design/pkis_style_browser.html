The Typical Set

In high dimensions, the mode carries vanishing probability. The typical set is where the mass actually concentrates.

A Gaussian's peak density sits at the mean, yet almost no samples land near it: the volume of that neighborhood shrinks faster than its density grows. Sampling instead targets the product of density and volume — the thin shell at radius √d. This is why concentration of measure governs MCMC mixing, and why it rhymes with the asymptotic equipartition property from information theory.
