Typical Set
pkis:concept:typical-set coverage 4/5 understanding 3/5 updated 2d
bayesian-statsinfo-theory

Definition

The set of outcomes whose empirical entropy is close to the source entropy — and which, despite each being individually improbable, together carry essentially all the probability mass.

Intuition

A Gaussian's peak density sits at the mean, yet almost no samples land near it: the volume of that neighborhood shrinks faster than its density grows. Sampling targets the product of density and volume — the thin shell at radius √d. This is why concentration of measure governs MCMC mixing.

The Typical Set

In high dimensions, the mode carries vanishing probability. The typical set is where the mass actually concentrates.

Walk the dimension up and watch the shell pull away from the center. The most probable single point becomes a measure-zero trap — high density, vanishing volume.

def typical_radius(d, sigma=1.0): return sigma * sqrt(d) # the shell, not the mode
