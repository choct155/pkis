The principle that two portfolios (or securities) with identical payoffs in all states of the world must have the same price; any violation implies a riskless profit opportunity (a "money machine") that cannot persist in a competitive market, and this constraint — weaker than full equilibrium — determines asset prices and underlies the MM theorems, put-call parity, and Black-Scholes option pricing.
