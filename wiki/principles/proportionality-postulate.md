The proportionality postulate is Congdon's claim that in equilibrium, percentage changes in broad money and percentage changes in nominal national income (and, separately, in the value of variable-income assets) are equi-proportionate — that is, money velocity is stable — qualified by the long-run "financialisation" tendency for financial transactions to grow faster than income.

Classification note: assigned as principle but may be result because Congdon presents empirical evidence (Tables 7 and 8, decadal US data 1960–2021) that stability holds sufficiently for the claim to function as an empirical regularity.
