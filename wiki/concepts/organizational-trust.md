Trust as the mechanism enabling cooperation in large organizations where repeated-game reputation effects are too weak — due to infrequent pairwise interaction — to sustain cooperation without an underlying prior disposition toward cooperative behavior.

## Reading Path
- [[laporta-trust-large-orgs-1996]] (unread) — theoretical argument and cross-country evidence that trust matters more for large organizations precisely because members interact infrequently
- [[rosenthal-lead-siloed-org-2024]] (unread) — presents lateral trust between executive peers as the mechanism enabling cross-functional collaboration; trust is built through proactive relationship investment and connective skills, not assumed
