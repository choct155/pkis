The fundamental organizational choice between coordinating economic activity through decentralized price signals (market) versus centralized authority and direction (hierarchy/firm); Coase (1937) establishes that transaction costs of the price mechanism determine where this boundary falls.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may be framework because the market-vs-hierarchy distinction organizes an entire research program in organizational economics and institutional theory.
