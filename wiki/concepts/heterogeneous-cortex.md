The claim that different cortical areas implement qualitatively distinct computational primitives — rather than repeating a single canonical microcircuit — supported by evidence from cytoarchitectonics (differential interneuron type distributions), gene expression profiling, and long-range connectivity patterns. Marcus, Marblestone & Dean (2014) argue that heterogeneity is the norm: PV-containing fast-spiking basket cells dominate V1 (~75%) while CR-containing neurons dominate PFC (~45%), reflecting area-specific computational specialization. This contrasts with "canonical microcircuit" theories (e.g., Douglas & Martin) that posit a single repeated circuit template across all cortical areas. Classification note: assigned as concept (a defined theoretical claim about cortical organization) but also_type result because it is an empirical claim grounded in anatomical and molecular data.
