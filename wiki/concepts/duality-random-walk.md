## Definition
Two stopping times tau and eta of an iid sequence are dual if for every n the event that n is a renewal epoch generated by iterates of tau equals the event that, looking backward from n (under the reversal map r_n), no iterate of eta is seen: {n in M_tau} = {n < eta o r_n}. The fundamental example is that the ascending ladder epoch N is dual to the descending ladder epoch Nbar.
