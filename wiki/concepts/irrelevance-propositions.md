A class of theoretical results in economics and finance showing that a seemingly consequential decision has no effect on the relevant outcome under specified conditions; their value lies not in proving literal irrelevance but in precisely identifying which frictions, if present, would make the decision matter — the MM theorems are the canonical examples.
