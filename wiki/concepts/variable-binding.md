The cognitive and computational operation of transiently associating a symbolic role (a variable, such as "subject" or "agent") with a specific instantiation (a filler, such as a particular word, concept, or object). Variable binding is proposed by Marcus, Marblestone & Dean (2014) as one of the fundamental "atoms of neural computation" likely absent in standard deep learning architectures. Four candidate neural mechanisms are discussed: PFC/BG indirection (gating of working memory registers via basal ganglia), dynamically partitionable autoassociative networks (DPANN), vector symbolic architectures (circular convolution of role and filler vectors), and binding through temporal synchrony.
