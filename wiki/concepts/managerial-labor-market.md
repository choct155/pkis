The competitive market for managerial services in which managers' wages are continuously revised based on performance signals from capital markets and internal monitoring, creating implicit ex post settling-up that disciplines managers without requiring them to hold residual claims; Fama (1980) argues this market is the primary mechanism explaining why separation of ownership and control is efficient.
