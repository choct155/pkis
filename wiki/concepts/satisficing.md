Satisficing (Simon 1955) is the decision principle by which cognitively bounded agents choose the first alternative that meets a pre-specified aspiration-level threshold rather than searching exhaustively for the globally optimal alternative; Cyert et al. (1959) operationalise this in a duopoly simulation model as the stopping rule for organisational search.
