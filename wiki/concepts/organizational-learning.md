Organizational learning is the process by which firms systematically update their beliefs about competitors' behaviour, market demand, and costs based on experience; in Cyert et al. (1959) it is operationalised as adaptive conjectural variation updating — each firm revises its perception of rivals' output responses using simple learning rules derived from observed market data.
