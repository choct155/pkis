Marcus, Marblestone & Dean's (2014) proposed research program for cataloguing elementary, reusable computational operations ("atoms") that underlie the diversity of cortical computation — analogous to a microprocessor's instruction set. The taxonomy includes: rapid perceptual classification, complex spatiotemporal pattern recognition, working memory, decision making, winner-take-all selection, routing of information flow, gain control, sequencing, and variable binding. The central argument is that the cortex is not uniform (contra canonical microcircuit theories) but implements diverse computational primitives that biological evolution programmed via area-specific molecular and developmental rules. Classification note: assigned as concept because it is primarily a research program proposal (an idea about how to systematize neuroscience), with also_type framework because it provides an organizing system for cross-area cortical computation.
