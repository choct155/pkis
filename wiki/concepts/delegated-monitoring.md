Delegated monitoring (Diamond 1984) is the theory that banks resolve the information asymmetry between dispersed savers and risky borrowers by monitoring borrowers on behalf of savers; by pooling many loans the bank diversifies idiosyncratic risk, and by financing itself with essentially safe debt the bank gains the right incentive to monitor — depositors' ability to force bankruptcy disciplines the bank to properly screen and monitor borrowers.
