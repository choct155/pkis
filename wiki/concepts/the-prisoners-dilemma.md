## Definition
The canonical non-cooperative game (Tucker 1950, after Flood and Dresher) illustrating the gap between individual and collective rationality. Two suspects independently choose to testify (defect) or refuse (cooperate); defection is a dominant strategy for each, so the unique dominant-strategy and Nash equilibrium is mutual defection — yet mutual cooperation Pareto-dominates it and maximizes both utilitarian and egalitarian social welfare. The 'dilemma' is that the strongest solution concept leads to the only outcome that fails every test of social desirability, with no way for rational independent agents to reach the better outcome. Escapes require changing the game: binding agreements (cooperative game), repetition (see folk theorem), or modified utility functions encoding fairness/morality.
