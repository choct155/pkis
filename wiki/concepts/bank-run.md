A bank run is a self-fulfilling coordination failure in which depositors, rationally anticipating that other depositors will withdraw, all rush to withdraw simultaneously — potentially collapsing even a fundamentally solvent institution because premature liquidation of illiquid assets cannot fully meet all obligations; Diamond and Dybvig (1983) provided the first rigorous microeconomic model of this phenomenon.

- [[allen-morris-game-theory-finance-2001]] (unread) — extends Diamond-Dybvig: shows that bank runs can be determined by higher-order beliefs (what depositors believe others believe about fundamentals) rather than fundamentals alone or sunspots; unique equilibrium result from correlated signals
