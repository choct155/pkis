Maturity transformation is the process by which financial intermediaries take short-maturity liquid liabilities (demand deposits) and create long-maturity illiquid assets (loans), thereby producing liquidity for the economy but simultaneously creating the structural vulnerability to self-fulfilling bank runs that Diamond and Dybvig (1983) formalised.
