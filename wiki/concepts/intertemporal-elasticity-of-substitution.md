The elasticity of the growth rate of consumption with respect to the real interest rate; equivalently, the willingness of a household to substitute consumption across time in response to changes in the rate of return; under standard CRRA utility, IES = 1/risk_aversion, linking two conceptually distinct preferences — under Epstein-Zin, they are separated, which is empirically important because IES > 1 implies the consumption-wealth ratio is not constant and that higher expected returns raise (rather than lower) current saving.
