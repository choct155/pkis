The role of the entrepreneur as the authority who replaces the price mechanism within a firm, directing factors of production within contractually specified limits; Coase (1937) argues this role exists because it economizes on the transaction costs of contracting through the market.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may be principle because the notion that entrepreneurial direction is a legitimate and necessary substitute for price-based coordination functions as a guiding constraint on organizational theory.
