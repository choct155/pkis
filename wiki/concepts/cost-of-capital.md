The required rate of return that a firm must earn on its investments to satisfy its investors' expectations given the risk of those investments; under the MM theorem, the cost of capital equals the capitalization rate for the firm's risk class and is independent of leverage — increasing debt raises equity cost by exactly enough to leave the weighted average unchanged.
