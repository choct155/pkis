## Definition
Datalog is the language of first-order definite clauses with no function symbols. It is named for its ability to express the kinds of statements made in relational databases; the absence of function symbols bounds the number of derivable ground facts, making inference decidable and forward chaining polynomial in data complexity (terminating, unlike the general first-order case).
