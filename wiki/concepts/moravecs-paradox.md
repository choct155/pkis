The observation (Moravec 1988, 1999; also Minsky 1986; Pinker 1994) that tasks which are effortless for human beings — such as understanding language, recognizing faces, or navigating physical space — are among the hardest to reverse-engineer for AI, while high-level reasoning tasks (chess, theorem proving) that seem sophisticated are comparatively easier to automate. The paradox reflects that effortless human skills embody millions of years of evolutionary optimization, while explicit reasoning is a recent and comparatively thin veneer. Dentella et al. (2024) invoke Moravec's paradox to frame why LLMs that succeed at medical/legal question answering fail at simple language comprehension.
