A class of probabilistic simulation models of human physical reasoning that posit subjects use Newtonian mechanics plus perceptual noise: the "noisy" element corresponds to uncertainty in the initial conditions (positions, velocities) of the physical system, handled via Monte Carlo sampling over possible trajectories. Associated with work by Sanborn, Mansinghka & Griffiths (2013) and Battaglia, Hamrick & Tenenbaum (2013). Davis and Marcus (2015) argue that noisy Newton models are too strong (predicting better performance than humans actually exhibit in many tasks) and fail to account for large systematic errors that cannot be attributed to perceptual noise alone.
