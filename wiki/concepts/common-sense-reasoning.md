Common-sense reasoning is the capacity to apply broad, stable, everyday background knowledge — about objects, actions, space, time, causality, and social conventions — to novel situations without explicit instruction; it encompasses both a large fact base (e.g., "you can't be in two places at once") and qualitative models of how the world works (e.g., "if it rains, uncovered outdoor items get wet"), and is the primary cognitive capacity that LLMs lack and that symbolic AI systems (Cyc, naive physics) aim to encode.
