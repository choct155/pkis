## Definition
An outcome is Pareto optimal (Pareto efficient) if there is no other outcome that makes one player better off without making someone else worse off. It is the least contentious social-welfare criterion: a non-Pareto-optimal outcome 'wastes utility' because more could be given to at least one agent at no cost to others. Pareto optimality constrains but does not uniquely determine a good outcome (many distributions can be Pareto optimal). It contrasts with aggregate criteria such as utilitarian social welfare (sum of utilities — ignores distribution and assumes a common utility scale) and egalitarian welfare (e.g., maximin over players, or the Gini coefficient). In the prisoner's dilemma, the unique Nash equilibrium is the only outcome that is *not* Pareto optimal.
