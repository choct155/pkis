A class of utility functions (due to Kreps and Porteus, 1978; Epstein and Zin, 1989) that generalizes expected utility by breaking the tight link between risk aversion and the intertemporal elasticity of substitution, allowing a household to simultaneously exhibit high aversion to cross-state risk and high willingness to substitute consumption across time, while also encoding a preference for the timing of resolution of uncertainty.
