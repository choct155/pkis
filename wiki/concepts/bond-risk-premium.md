The excess expected return on a long-term bond over rolling short-term bonds of equal maturity, compensating investors for bearing interest rate risk, inflation uncertainty, and the risk of unfavorable changes in the discount rate over the holding period; an upward-sloping average yield curve reflects a positive term premium, and understanding its structural sources — risk aversion, inflation-SDF correlation, time-varying uncertainty — is a central challenge in macro-finance.
