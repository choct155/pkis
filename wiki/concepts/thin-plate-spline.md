## Definition
The multivariate generalization of the one-dimensional cubic smoothing spline. For x_i ∈ ℝ^d one minimizes Σ(y_i−f(x_i))² + λ J[f] where J is a rotation-invariant roughness penalty; in ℝ² the canonical penalty is J[f]=∫∫[(∂²f/∂x_1²)² + 2(∂²f/∂x_1∂x_2)² + (∂²f/∂x_2²)²] dx_1 dx_2. The minimizer is finite-dimensional and has the form f(x)=β_0+βᵀx+Σ_j α_j h_j(x) with radial basis functions h_j(x)=‖x−x_j‖² log‖x−x_j‖ centered at the data points, the α_j obeying linear constraints so the penalty stays finite. Like the 1-D smoothing spline, λ→0 gives an interpolant, λ→∞ the least-squares plane, and intermediate λ a generalized-ridge fit; λ is again set via df_λ=trace(S_λ). Computation is O(N³) (no sparse structure), but using a K-knot lattice reduces it to O(NK²+K³). Thin-plate splines fall inside the RKHS regularization framework — they are an expansion in radial basis functions generated by the kernel K(x,y)=‖x−y‖² log‖x−y‖ — and coincide with the kriging estimate of a Gaussian random field in spatial statistics.
