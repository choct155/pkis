The dispositional component of trust: the degree to which trusting behavior stems from stable individual preferences — risk tolerance, reciprocity norms, altruism — rather than beliefs about others' trustworthiness, better captured by questions about past trusting behavior than by WVS-style attitudinal questions.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may share characteristics with a principle because preference-based trust functions as a baseline disposition that constrains behavioral choices analogously to how principles constrain reasoning.
