Defeasibility is the property of a reasoning system's conclusions that they are default-true — reached in the absence of contradicting information — and can be retracted when new, contradicting evidence arrives; defeasible reasoning requires the system to maintain belief revision mechanisms, track the provenance of each conclusion, and actively update or inform others when prior answers are retracted.
