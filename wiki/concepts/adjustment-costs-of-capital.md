The idea that installing or removing capital is costly beyond its purchase price, making investment smoother and slower to respond to changes in expected returns than a frictionless model would predict; in quantitative DSGE models the Jermann (1998) specification represents installation costs as a concave function of the investment-capital ratio, introducing a wedge between the price of capital goods and the value of capital in production, and generating a more realistic stochastic discount factor with amplified fluctuations.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may also be technique because applied macroeconomists "install" adjustment costs as a specific functional-form device in their models, with the adjustment cost parameter estimated from data.
