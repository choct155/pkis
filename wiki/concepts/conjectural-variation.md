A conjectural variation is a firm's belief about how its rivals will respond to changes in its own output or price; in neoclassical oligopoly theory these are taken as given (zero in Cournot, negative one in Bertrand), but in Cyert et al.'s behavioral model they are adaptive — updated each period via organisational learning from observed competitor behaviour.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may be technique because the conjectural variation updating rule in Cyert et al. functions as a specific computational procedure (observe rival output, compute deviation from expectation, update belief by fraction).
