## Definition
A criterion for evaluating outcomes from the perspective of society as a whole, adopting the viewpoint of a benevolent omniscient observer choosing the best collective outcome. Utilitarian social welfare is the sum of players' utilities — simple but distribution-blind and dependent on a common interpersonal utility scale (the 'utility monster' problem). Egalitarian social welfare instead targets the distribution, e.g. maximizing the worst-off player's utility (a maximin approach) or minimizing inequality (Gini coefficient); these can sacrifice large total welfare for small distributional gains. In social choice theory, a social-welfare *function* maps each voter's preference order to a collective preference ranking (vs. a social-choice function, which outputs only a winner set).
