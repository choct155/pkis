Bounded rationality (Herbert Simon) is the theory that agents make decisions under cognitive and information constraints that prevent full optimisation; rather than searching exhaustively over all alternatives and selecting the global optimum, bounded-rational agents use heuristics and aspiration-level satisficing rules that are computationally and informationally feasible.
