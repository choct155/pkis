The credit channel (Bernanke 1983) is the mechanism by which bank failures cause real economic harm through the destruction of lending relationships and screening expertise, distinct from — and additional to — the monetary-supply contraction channel emphasised by Friedman and Schwartz; the disruption of credit intermediation deepens and prolongs recessions by cutting off households, farms, and small businesses from funding.
