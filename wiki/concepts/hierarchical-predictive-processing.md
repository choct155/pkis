A neuroscience framework (Clark, Friston) in which the brain constructs hierarchical generative models that generate top-down predictions of sensory inputs and refine them through bottom-up prediction-error signals — under this account, causal models emerge as a consequence of iterative error minimization, and active inference (taking actions to confirm predictions) creates a causal perception-action loop.

Classification note: assigned as concept because hierarchical predictive processing names a specific mechanistic account of brain function; also typed as framework because it provides an organizational scheme for understanding cognition across modalities and levels of abstraction.
