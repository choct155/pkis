The hypothesis that financial markets are efficient if it is impossible to earn economic profits by trading on available information — implying that asset prices reflect all available information and follow a random walk; its corporate finance implication is that market value is an unbiased estimate of the present value of expected cash flows and that managers should maximize current market value.
