Inflation driven by expectations that the government will not generate sufficient future primary surpluses to back outstanding nominal debt, causing agents to dump money and bonds today; can occur well before large deficits materialize and is likely to come with stagnation rather than a boom, since it shifts the Phillips curve rather than raising aggregate demand.
