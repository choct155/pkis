The proposition that rational households treat deficit-financed government spending equivalently to tax-financed spending, because they anticipate future taxes to repay the debt; implies that fiscal deficits have no stimulative effect on aggregate demand.

Classification note: assigned as concept but also qualifies as result since it is derived as a theorem under specific utility-maximization and market completeness assumptions (Barro 1974).

## Reading Path
- [[cochrane-unpleasant-fiscal-2010]] (unread) — provides an important extension: with nominal (rather than real) government debt, Ricardian equivalence can fail rationally (without market failure) because agents may rationally expect monetization rather than future taxes; prospective deficits are thus as stimulative as current deficits in the FTPL framework
