The monetary transmission mechanism describes the channels through which changes in the money supply produce changes in real and nominal economic outcomes; Congdon's restatement emphasises variable-income asset prices (equities, real estate) as the primary channel, in contrast to the Keynesian emphasis on bond yields and the interest rate.

## Reading Path
- [[congdon-quantity-theory-2024]] (unread) — central to chapters 5 and 6; argues that asset-price effects dominate the interest-rate channel and that ignoring variable-income assets is the key error of New Keynesian models
- [[fama-fed-interest-rates-2013]] (unread) — provides empirical evidence that Fed control attenuates rapidly along the term structure, casting doubt on the interest-rate channel as the primary transmission mechanism
