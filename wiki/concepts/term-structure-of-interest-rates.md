The relationship between interest rates (or bond yields) and time to maturity, typically represented by the yield curve; the term structure encodes expectations of future short rates, inflation, and risk premia, and is the transmission channel through which central bank policy propagates to long-term investment decisions.

## Reading Path
- [[fama-fed-interest-rates-2013]] (unread) — provides empirical evidence that the Fed's control over interest rates attenuates rapidly along the term structure; the 5-year and 10-year rates show weak systematic response to FF target changes, dominated instead by demand for safe assets and risk premia
- [[binsbergen-term-structure-dsge-2011]] (unread) — structural, production-economy DSGE account of the nominal yield curve estimated by ML; quantifies the tension between matching the term premium and inflation dynamics, and traces both to the correlation structure between the SDF (driven by technology shocks) and inflation innovations
