The market process by which dispersed private information becomes incorporated into publicly observable asset prices through the trading activity of informed participants; Cochrane (2013) argues that high-frequency information trading facilitates this process, and that many market "inefficiencies" and crisis episodes reflect too little rather than too much active trading and price discovery.
