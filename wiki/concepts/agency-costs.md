The costs arising from incentive conflicts in contractual relationships: monitoring expenditures by the principal, bonding expenditures by the agent, and the residual loss from actions that cannot be cost-effectively enforced; Jensen and Meckling (1976) establish that these costs are borne by the parties to the contract at formation, giving all parties incentive to minimize them.
