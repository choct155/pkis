Monetary equilibrium is the state in which non-bank private sector agents' desired holdings of broad money exactly equal the quantity of money created by the banking system, determining the nominal level of national income and wealth; departures from this equilibrium ("monetary disequilibrium") drive adjustments in prices and asset values until equilibrium is restored.
