Bank fragility is the structural vulnerability of financial intermediaries arising from maturity transformation: because demand deposits are withdrawable on demand while loans are illiquid and long-term, even a fundamentally solvent bank cannot survive a sufficiently large and rapid withdrawal of deposits, making self-fulfilling panics possible and systemic crises a recurring feature of unregulated financial systems.
