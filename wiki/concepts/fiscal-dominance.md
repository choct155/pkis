A macroeconomic regime in which fiscal policy dominates monetary policy, forcing the central bank to accommodate all Treasury financing needs and surrender operational independence, with the consequence that price-level control transfers from monetary to fiscal authorities.

## Reading Path
- [[castaneda-mmt-critique-2021]] (unread) — characterises fiscal dominance as the inevitable endpoint of full MMT adoption; argues it historically produces entrenched inflation by eliminating the central bank's anti-inflationary anchor
- [[cochrane-unpleasant-fiscal-2010]] (unread) — provides the formal FTPL mechanism for fiscal dominance: when the fiscal valuation equation drives the price level, the central bank's control over inflation collapses regardless of its balance sheet management
