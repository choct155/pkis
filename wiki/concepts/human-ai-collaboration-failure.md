The empirically documented phenomenon, established across 106 experimental studies, in which human-AI teams frequently underperform the best individual performer — with the magnitude of under-performance moderated by task type (decision-making tasks show significant losses; creative tasks show gains) and relative ability (when AI outperforms humans independently, collaboration yields significant losses, g = −0.54).

Classification note: assigned as concept because it names a specific, defined phenomenon with measurable boundary conditions and known moderators; also typed as problem because it is the primary motivating challenge for designing task-appropriate human-AI collaboration frameworks.
