The board of directors viewed as a market-induced institution serving as the ultimate internal monitor of a firm's top management, whose composition (inside vs. outside directors) and effectiveness are shaped by competitive pressures from managerial and director labor markets; Fama (1980) argues a board dominated by competing top managers with outside "professional referee" directors may be more effective than one dominated by diffuse shareholders.

Classification note: assigned as concept but may be framework because the specific theory of how boards should be structured and monitored organizes a governance research program.
