Develops the structure theory of linear maps: the Jordan–Chevalley theorem (additive and multiplicative decompositions into semisimple and nilpotent parts), the Jordan canonical form (Jordan blocks, string bases, nilpotent endomorphisms), and the connection between Jordan form and the minimal polynomial.
