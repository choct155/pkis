## Summary
David MacKay's unified treatment of information theory, Bayesian inference, and learning algorithms (2003). Three threads woven together: data compression and noisy-channel coding (Part I), probabilistic inference and Monte Carlo / variational methods (Part II), and neural networks and sparse-graph codes (Parts III-IV). Freely available; a canonical bridge between the Bayesian-computation and coding-theory clusters.
