Introduces Pearl's DAG framework as a practitioner's graphical language for causal identification: nodes, directed edges, confounders, colliders, the backdoor criterion, and d-separation — illustrated via labor economics examples (college education, gender wage gap) with Stata simulation to demonstrate collider bias and correct covariate adjustment.
