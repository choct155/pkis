## Summary
Position paper arguing that knowledge graph infrastructure is a compounding investment that simultaneously reduces three distinct cost lines: retrieval cost (amortization), quality measurement cost (passive instrumentation + PPI++), and content validation cost (structural constraints). Each line is independently justifiable; together they constitute a structural argument for graph-based architecture as the dominant approach for AI-powered data products at scale. The argument is analytical: key quantities are derivable, crossover points are computable, and the improvement trajectory is a mathematical consequence of the architecture. Draft v0.1 — evolving as empirical validation from the intelligent layer project produces real numbers.

## Key Knowledge Objects
Five core arguments: (1) amortization result — Eff(q,n) = I(q;n)/tokens(n) dominance of concept nodes; (2) hardening trajectory — continuous λ(n,t) mixture from LLM fallback to graph retrieval; (3) quality measurement framework — seven dimensions, five computable from graph structure; (4) content quality — completeness, validity, compositional coherence as structural facts; (5) compounding returns — three cost lines improve simultaneously and additively over time.
