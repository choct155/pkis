The quantity theory of money is a macroeconomic framework asserting that changes in the stock of money are the primary determinant of the price level and nominal income in equilibrium, formalised through the equation of exchange (MV = PT) and operationalised via empirical relationships between broad money growth and nominal GDP growth.
