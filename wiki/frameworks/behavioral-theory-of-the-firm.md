The behavioral theory of the firm (Carnegie School; Cyert, March, Simon) is a framework that replaces the neoclassical profit-maximisation assumption with a decision-process account of business organisations: firms are coalitions of participants with potentially conflicting goals, their decision procedures incorporate satisficing, aspiration-level revision, organisational slack, and adaptive learning rather than optimisation over a known choice set.
