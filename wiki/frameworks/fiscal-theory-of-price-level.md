A macroeconomic framework in which the price level is determined by the government debt valuation equation — the real value of nominal government debt must equal the present value of expected future primary surpluses — rather than primarily by the money supply; implies that fiscal expectations drive current inflation and that open market operations (swapping money for bonds) are price-level neutral.
