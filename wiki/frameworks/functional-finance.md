Abba Lerner's doctrine that fiscal policy should be evaluated solely by its macroeconomic effects — full employment, price stability — rather than by analogy to household budget constraints; government should spend as much as needed to achieve macroeconomic goals and tax only to regulate aggregate demand and reduce private net financial assets.
