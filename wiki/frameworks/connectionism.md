## Definition
Connectionism is a paradigm in cognitive science and AI holding that **intelligent behavior emerges from large networks of simple, neuron-like processing units** interacting through weighted connections, without explicit symbolic rules.

Key tenets: (1) knowledge is stored in connection weights, not in explicit symbols; (2) learning is weight adjustment via experience; (3) distributed representations spread information across many units.

### Why it matters
The 1980s–1990s connectionist movement revived neural network research after the first AI winter, introduced backpropagation training for multi-layer networks, and established distributed representations and parallel distributed processing as core principles — all of which directly underpin modern deep learning.

### Major contributions
- Popularization of backpropagation (Rumelhart et al., 1986)
- Distributed representations (Hinton et al., 1986)
- Sequence modeling precursors leading to LSTM (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997)
