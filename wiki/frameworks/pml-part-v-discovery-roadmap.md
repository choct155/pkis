## Definition
A structured curriculum for probabilistic discovery methods that proceeds through four levels of data complexity:

1. **Fixed-dimensional vectors** $x \in \mathbb{R}^D$ — standard latent variable models (Ch. 28).
2. **Sequences** $x = x_{1:T}$ (speech, video, genomics) — sequential latent variable models, linear and nonlinear (Ch. 29).
3. **General graphs** — graph-structured latent variable models (Ch. 30).
4. **Nonparametric Bayesian models** — models whose complexity (number of hidden states, functional form) grows with data (Ch. 31).
5. **Representation learning with neural networks** (Ch. 32) and **model interpretation** (Ch. 33).

### Why it matters
The roadmap clarifies how increasing structural complexity of data calls for richer model families, and how nonparametric priors maintain interpretability while allowing model complexity to adapt to data quantity and quality.
