A family of structural macroeconomic models that characterize aggregate dynamics as the general equilibrium outcome of optimizing households and firms facing well-specified preferences, technology, and constraints, with all agents sharing rational expectations; the framework imposes cross-equation restrictions across all endogenous variables simultaneously, making it suitable for structural estimation, welfare analysis, and policy counterfactuals that are inaccessible to reduced-form time-series models.
