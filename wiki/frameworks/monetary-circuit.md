The monetary circuit (Graziani 2003 and the Italian/French post-Keynesian tradition) is a macroeconomic framework in which money is created ex nihilo by banks at the start of each production period when firms borrow to pay wages, circulates through the economy as purchases are made, and is destroyed at the period's end when firms repay loans; it differs from MMT primarily in that private banking — not state spending — is the originating locus of money creation.

Classification note: assigned as framework but may be concept because "monetary circuit" names both the theoretical approach and the actual circular flow of credit money through a production economy.
