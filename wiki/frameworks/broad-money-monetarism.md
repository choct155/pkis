Broad-money monetarism is the position — associated principally with Tim Congdon — that a broadly defined money aggregate (bank deposits inclusive, M3 or equivalent) rather than monetary base or narrow money is the relevant causal variable for nominal income and inflation determination; it differs from Chicago School monetarism by rejecting the reliability of the base-money multiplier and denying that central bank liabilities are the direct driver of economic outcomes.

Classification note: assigned as framework but may be concept because it describes a theoretical stance rather than a complete system of methods; the underlying machinery is the quantity theory of money with broad-money as the operative variable.
