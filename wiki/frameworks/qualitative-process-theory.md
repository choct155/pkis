Qualitative Process (QP) Theory, introduced by Forbus (1984, MIT), is a knowledge representation framework for commonsense physical reasoning that takes *physical processes* (flowing, boiling, stretching, motion) as the primitive ontological category causing change; quantities are represented in Quantity Spaces (partially ordered symbolic values), and processes are specified by their individuals, preconditions, quantity conditions, relations, and influences — enabling qualitative proofs about complex physical behavior (e.g., limit cycle existence in oscillators) without numerical simulation.
