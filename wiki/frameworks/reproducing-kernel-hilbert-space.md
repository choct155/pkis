## Definition
A Hilbert space H_K of functions generated by a positive-definite kernel K(x,y), equipped with an inner product under which evaluation is reproduced: for f∈H_K, ⟨K(·,x_i), f⟩_{H_K}=f(x_i) (the reproducing property), so K(·,x_i) is the *representer of evaluation* at x_i. It is the function space underlying a general class of regularization problems min_{f∈H} Σ L(y_i, f(x_i)) + λ J(f). With J(f)=‖f‖²_{H_K}, an eigen-expansion K(x,y)=Σ γ_i φ_i(x)φ_i(y) (γ_i≥0) lets elements be written f=Σ c_i φ_i with norm ‖f‖²_{H_K}=Σ c_i²/γ_i, so the penalty acts as a **generalized ridge**: components with large kernel eigenvalues ("smooth" eigenfunctions) are penalized less, rough ones more.

The defining payoff is that although the criterion lives on an infinite-dimensional space, its minimizer is finite-dimensional, f(x)=Σ_{i=1}^N α_i K(x,x_i) (the representer theorem; ESL Ex. 5.15), and J(f)=Σ_{ij} K(x_i,x_j)α_iα_j, reducing the problem to the N-dimensional min_α L(y, Kα) + λ αᵀKα — the *kernel property* exploited by support vector machines. For squared-error loss this gives α̂=(K+λI)⁻¹y, the kriging / Gaussian-random-field estimate. Choices of K instantiate many methods: the polynomial kernel (⟨x,y⟩+1)^d spans degree-d polynomials; the Gaussian kernel e^{−ν‖x−y‖²} yields a Gaussian radial-basis expansion; ‖x−y‖² log‖x−y‖ yields the thin-plate spline. Smoothing and thin-plate splines are special cases (with a null space H_0 of unpenalized low-degree polynomials, H=H_0⊕H_1). There is a Bayesian reading: f is a zero-mean Gaussian process with prior covariance K, the penalty being the prior's contribution to the joint likelihood.
