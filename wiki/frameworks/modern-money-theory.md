Modern Money Theory (MMT) is a heterodox macroeconomic framework asserting that a monetarily sovereign government (which issues its own currency and lets it float) faces no nominal financing constraint, can always fund spending through central bank monetisation, and should direct fiscal policy to achieve full employment via an Employer of Last Resort programme rather than constraining expenditure to match tax receipts.
