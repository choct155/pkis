## Connection
Organizational context for ontologist positioning at a private markets data organization. Three groups — KG, QMI, Operations — have been operating as siloed product teams with shallow cross-group understanding despite complementary capabilities.

Key structural observations:
- KG owns fields management and semantic inventory management (operational accountabilities with throughput). Moving toward signal interpretation and quality arbitration away from model authoring.
- QMI (data quality, BI, process engineering) owns measurement, reporting, analytical access. Being pushed toward actionable analytical intelligence, composable data products, and operations research respectively.
- All four trajectories converge on the same underlying capability: using structured analytical methods to make decisions about complex systems.

Organizational pressure: one manager is planning an internal rework that would ossify current group boundaries before cross-group collaboration can be established. Timing constraint is acute — waiting risks structural lock-in.

Two organizing logics under consideration:
1. Capability-based: semantic modeling, measurement, delivery cutting across current group lines
2. Product/deliverable-based: each product pulls the capabilities it needs

The intelligent layer project is the natural convergence substrate — neither KG nor QMI can build the hardening framework independently. Joint accountability for graph construction quality AND measurement of downstream impact on content hydration cost and quality is the recommended starting point.

Strategic framing for senior stakeholders: scalable content hydration for AI-powered client-facing products and automation for vendorized workforce. Intelligent layer is experimental substrate, not the deliverable.

The ontologist checker-vs-creator objection mirrors software engineers' live coding objection. Resolution: high signal-to-noise ratio on the decision queue is what determines whether semantic modelers experience this as creative expert work or as reviewing LLM output. The system must be disciplined about what it sends to the human.

Deferred: specific talent movement decisions, manager promotion implications, final organizing logic choice.
