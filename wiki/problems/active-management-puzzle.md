The puzzle that high-fee actively managed investment funds have persisted for decades despite evidence that the average fund earns zero alpha (before fees) and negative alpha (after fees), which cannot be explained by simple investor irrationality given the 40-year time span; Berk-Green (2004) resolves it via a supply-demand model where manager alpha has diminishing returns to scale and managers capture all alpha in fees, leaving investors with competitive returns.
