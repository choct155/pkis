## Definition
The situation (Lloyd 1833; Hardin 1968) in which a shared resource that no one must pay to use is over-exploited, yielding lower total utility for everyone than coordinated restraint would. Structurally it is a multi-player prisoner's dilemma: continuing to exploit (e.g., pollute) is a dominant strategy for each agent, but universal exploitation leaves all worse off than universal restraint, with no mechanism for rational agents to reach the better outcome under the current rules. Remedies operate by making *externalities* explicit — charging agents for use of the commons (e.g., a carbon tax, or a VCG-style payment) so that each agent's local utility-maximizing decision also maximizes global utility. Coase (1960) and Ostrom (1990) offer property-rights and community-governance alternatives.
