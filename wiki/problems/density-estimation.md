## Definition
Density estimation is the general task of learning a probability model from data that are assumed to have been generated by that model. Originally referring to probability density functions for continuous variables, the term is now used for discrete distributions too. It is a form of unsupervised learning and subsumes parameter learning (fitting numerical parameters of a fixed-structure model), structure learning (discovering the model's form), and nonparametric estimation.
