The equation of exchange (MV = PT, due to Irving Fisher) is the accounting identity that relates the money stock (M) multiplied by velocity (V) to the price level (P) multiplied by the volume of transactions (T); in national-income applications it is restated as MV = PY, where Y is real output, and forms the formal backbone of the quantity theory of money.
