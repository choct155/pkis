Under conditions of perfect capital markets (no taxes, no bankruptcy costs, symmetric information), a firm's total value is independent of its capital structure (Proposition I) and its equity cost rises linearly with leverage (Proposition II), proved by the no-arbitrage argument that investors can replicate any capital structure through personal borrowing.
