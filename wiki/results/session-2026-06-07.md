## Summary

A full-day depth session covering variational inference, MCMC, HMC, and their connections to graph-based LLM reasoning. The session moved from foundational Bayesian computation through to the variational graph traversal hypothesis, established the understanding assessment protocol, and initialized two new research directions: graph schema expressivity as a testable axis and graceful degradation under graph incompleteness. The most generative moments were the derivation of VGT as approximate posterior inference (independent of prompting), the identification of premature concentration as a cross-domain failure mode, and the epistemological anchor taxonomy for inference algorithms.

## Key Insights

The central insight about mean field approximation that had been missing was the distinction between constraining the search path versus constraining the search space. [[mean-field-approximation]] does not restrict how you move across the posterior landscape — it restricts which landscape you are allowed to search in the first place. The wind field analogy (agreeing to model wind as a uniform vector before optimization begins) made this precise in a way that will survive the session. This resolved a confusion that had persisted through prior reading of the VI paper.

The [[metropolis-algorithm]] became clear through a single reframe: the core mechanism is an odds ratio, and the normalizing constant cancels. This had been obscured by the island-hopping metaphor in prior exposures. The detailed balance argument then fell out naturally: the asymmetry in acceptance probabilities is exactly compensated by the asymmetry in visiting frequencies, so the flux between any two points is equal. Accepting worse proposals is probability matching, not optimization — a distinction that clarifies why MCMC is not doing gradient descent.

The variational graph traversal hypothesis emerged organically from the question of what makes beam search in [[sun-thinkongraph-2023]] a heuristic. Recognizing that beam search collapses the ELBO entropy term to a constant (log k) meant the traversal is purely fit-driven — the same failure mode as posterior collapse in VAEs, [[mean-field-approximation]] overestimating joint coverage, and policy collapse in RL. This cross-domain pattern became the [[bn-20260607-a-recurring-failure-mode-across]] (premature concentration) bridge note: a system with competing fit and entropy objectives fails when fit dominates. The variational formulation of graph traversal then followed directly, with beam width as variational family size and ELBO plateau as convergence criterion.

The epistemological anchor taxonomy [[bn-20260607-every-inference-algorithm-targeting-a]] emerged from comparing Gibbs sampling to CAVI. The key question was: why does Gibbs require exact conditional draws while CAVI tolerates approximate gradient steps? The answer is that CAVI has the ELBO as a third-party reference that corrects drift; Gibbs has no such reference. This reframed the differences between Metropolis-Hastings (likelihood ratio as anchor), Gibbs (analytic closure as anchor), CAVI (ELBO as anchor), and HMC (gradient + Hamiltonian conservation as anchor) into a unified taxonomy.

The connection between VI and policy gradient RL was identified as load-bearing for the VGT hypothesis but the foundations were not in place to fully ground it. The log derivative trick is the mathematical hinge — the score function estimator in VI and REINFORCE in RL are the same object. This gap drives the immediate reading priority: [[mohamed-monte-2020]] before [[sutton-policy-2018]].

The graph schema expressivity axis [[graph-schema-expressivity]] emerged from asking whether OWL typing gives the VGT scoring matrix an informative prior. The answer is yes — domain and range constraints partially pre-specify which relation types are relevant for which query categories, reducing the amount the matrix needs to learn from task outcomes. This is a uniquely testable claim in the IKS dual-graph architecture (GraphDB OWL-RL vs Neo4j LPG on the same content). The broader principle: schema expressivity is inversely proportional to the interpretive work the LLM must do during traversal.

The [[understanding-assessment-protocol]] was designed during the session. Key design decisions: scores are earned through demonstrated behavior, not self-assessed; node-level score = minimum of component scores (floor, not average); decay function operates on a per-level schedule; three ambient triggers fire continuously without requiring explicit calibration sessions.

## Open Questions

1. Is the policy gradient learning objective for the VGT scoring matrix mathematically equivalent to maximizing the ELBO, or only structurally analogous? Requires working through Mohamed et al. (2020) formally. Relevant nodes: [[variational-graph-traversal]], [[mohamed-monte-2020]]

2. What is the right prior over graph paths p(π) in the VGT ELBO formulation? Uniform? Degree-weighted? Learned from the ontology? This affects the entropy term behavior in sparse graphs. Relevant nodes: [[variational-graph-traversal]], [[graph-schema-expressivity]]

3. At what width does the mean field approximation of neural network layers become practically accurate, and how does this interact with depth? Relevant node: [[mean-field-approximation]]

4. Is there a unified formal account spanning statistical physics, VI, and neural network theory for mean field, or are these analogies rather than instances of a common mathematical structure? Unresolved in the literature. Relevant node: [[mean-field-approximation]]

5. What is the graph hardening threshold — the signal that triggers transition from zero-shot LLM scoring to learned VGT scoring matrix? Candidate signals: edge density, coverage score on key relation types, traversal success rate. Relevant node: [[graceful-degradation-graph-incompleteness]]

6. Does the particle filter approximation to Q(π) actually outperform beam search in practice, or does the entropy signal degrade in sparse graphs? Relevant nodes: [[variational-graph-traversal]], [[particle-filter]]
