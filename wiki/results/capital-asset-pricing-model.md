The equilibrium model (Treynor, Sharpe, Lintner, 1961–65) that prices assets by their systematic (covariance) risk alone: E(R_j) = RF + [E(RM) − RF] × β_j, where β_j is the covariance of asset j with the market portfolio divided by market variance; idiosyncratic risk is diversified away and earns no premium; CAPM defines the required rate of return (opportunity cost of capital) for corporate investment decisions.

Classification note: assigned as result (it is a specific derived equilibrium characterization) but is also a framework for organizing empirical asset pricing research.
