## Definition
A foundational impossibility result of social choice theory (Arrow 1951): for three or more outcomes, no social welfare function can simultaneously satisfy the Pareto condition, independence of irrelevant alternatives (IIA), and non-dictatorship (together with unrestricted domain / the Condorcet-winner condition). Any rule satisfying the reasonable-seeming conditions must violate at least one. The theorem does not imply democratic decision making is hopeless — it shows that for *any* chosen voting rule there exist (possibly pathological) preference profiles producing controversial or paradoxical outcomes.
