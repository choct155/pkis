## Definition
Let $f: \Omega \to \mathbb{R}$ be a strictly convex, continuously differentiable function. The **Bregman divergence** is:

$$B_f(w \| v) = f(w) - f(v) - (w-v)^T \nabla f(v) \geq 0$$

For exponential family distributions with natural parameters $\eta$ and log-partition function $A(\eta)$, the KL divergence equals the Bregman divergence generated by $A$:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_{\eta_q} \| p_{\eta_p}) = B_A(\eta_q \| \eta_p) = A(\eta_q) - A(\eta_p) - (\eta_q - \eta_p)^T \nabla A(\eta_p)$$

The KL divergence is the **unique** divergence that is simultaneously a Bregman divergence and an $f$-divergence.

### Why it matters
Embedding KL divergence in the Bregman family unifies it with squared Euclidean distance and Mahalanobis distance, reveals its geometric meaning as a gap from a linear approximation, and enables the mirror-descent / natural-gradient frameworks for optimization.
