## Definition
A negative result of social choice theory on strategic voting: any social choice function satisfying the Pareto condition over a domain with more than two outcomes is either *manipulable* or a *dictatorship*. That is, for any reasonable, non-dictatorial voting rule there exist circumstances in which a voter can benefit by misrepresenting their true preferences — no such rule can be made truth-revealing (strategy-proof). It is the social-choice analogue of the impossibility of a fully incentive-compatible voting mechanism, though it does not say how manipulation could be carried out or how likely it is in practice. (E.g., under plurality, a voter whose favorite cannot win may rationally vote for a second choice.)
