MM Proposition I: under perfect capital markets (no taxes, no bankruptcy costs, no information asymmetry), the total market value of a firm is determined solely by its expected earnings and risk class, not by the mix of debt and equity used to finance its assets; the result is proved via a homemade-leverage arbitrage argument.
