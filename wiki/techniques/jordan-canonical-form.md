The Jordan canonical form is the unique (up to permutation of blocks) block-diagonal matrix similar to a given square matrix, composed of Jordan blocks of the form λI + N (where N is nilpotent), realizing the Jordan–Chevalley decomposition into semisimple and nilpotent parts over algebraically closed fields.
