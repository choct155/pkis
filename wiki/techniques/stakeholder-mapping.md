A self-assessment practice in which a leader enumerates their top five to eight stakeholders and plots each on a two-dimensional matrix of (a) strategic importance to achieving one's objectives versus (b) current frequency of interaction, identifying mismatches that reveal under-invested relationships requiring deliberate attention.
