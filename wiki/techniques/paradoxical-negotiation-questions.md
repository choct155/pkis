A negotiation technique in which a leader frames a collaborative challenge with a question that simultaneously acknowledges both parties' objectives — e.g., "How can you and I find a solution that enables me to achieve what I'm trying to do, while also enabling your team to meet its objectives?" — forcing expansive, integrative thinking rather than zero-sum either/or framing.

Classification note: assigned as technique because it is a specific conversational procedure with identifiable inputs and outputs, but may also be a principle since it encodes a normative commitment to mutual-gain thinking that shapes how one approaches any negotiation, not just a discrete method one applies in isolation.
