## Definition
A function-estimation method that represents the signal in a **complete orthonormal wavelet basis** and then **shrinks and selects** coefficients toward a sparse representation — the complement to smoothing splines, which use a complete basis but impose smoothness via pure shrinkage. Wavelet bases are generated by translations and dilations of a scaling function (father) φ and a mother wavelet ψ: φ_{j,k}=2^{j/2}φ(2^j x−k), ψ_{j,k}=2^{j/2}ψ(2^j x−k). A **multiresolution analysis** nests reference spaces ···⊃V_1⊃V_0⊃V_{-1}··· with V_{j+1}=V_j⊕W_j (W_j the orthogonal-complement detail space), giving the decomposition V_J=V_0⊕W_0⊕···⊕W_{J−1}. The Haar basis (ψ=φ(2x)−φ(2x−1)) gives piecewise-constant detail; smoother families like the Daubechies symmlet-p have wider support (2p−1 intervals) and p vanishing moments (∫ψ(x)x^j dx=0, j<p), so order-p polynomials are reproduced exactly in V_0 (the analog of the smoothing-spline penalty null space).

The key virtue is simultaneous **time and frequency localization**: unlike the Fourier basis (frequency localization only), wavelets place basis functions where the signal needs detail, efficiently representing both smooth stretches and isolated spikes/bumps. Fitting uses the wavelet transform y*=Wᵀy (the full least-squares coefficients), then SURE shrinkage (Donoho–Johnstone): minimizing ‖y−Wθ‖² + 2λ‖θ‖_1 — identical to the lasso, and because W is orthonormal it solves in closed form by soft-thresholding θ̂_j = sign(y*_j)(|y*_j|−λ)_+, with a common choice λ=σ√(2 log N). The fit is the inverse transform f̂=Wθ̂. The L_1 penalty does shrinkage *and selection* (vs. the spline L_2 penalty's pure shrinkage). Pyramidal algorithms compute the transform in O(N), faster than the FFT's N log N; wavelets are central to signal processing and image compression.
