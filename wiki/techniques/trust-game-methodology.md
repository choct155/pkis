The Berg-Dickhaut-McCabe (1995) experimental paradigm in which a sender (first mover) chooses how much of an endowment to transfer to a receiver (second mover) — the transferred amount is multiplied (typically tripled) — and the receiver decides how much to return; the amount sent measures trust and the fraction returned measures trustworthiness, with extensions allowing incentivized belief elicitation to separate cognitive from preference-based determinants of trusting behavior.
